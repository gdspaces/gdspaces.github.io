he best Azure storage redundancy option for your company is Geo-redundant storage (GRS). GRS replicates your data three times within the primary region, and then synchronously replicates it to a secondary region that is hundreds of miles away. Selected Answer: B
RA-GRS allows Read-Only Access to the secondary region without the need for failure in the primary region. However, because data is replicated asynchronously from the primary to the secondary region, the secondary region is typically behind the primary region in terms of write operations. If a disaster were to strike the primary region, it's likely that some data would be lost. The interval between the most recent writes to the primary region and the last write to the secondary region is known as the recovery point objective (RPO). The RPO indicates the point in time to which data can be recovered. The Azure Storage platform typically has an RPO of less than 15 minutes, although there's currently no SLA on how long it takes to replicate data to the secondary region. And note that Azure Files does not support read-access geo-redundant storage (RA-GRS) or read-access geo-zone-redundant storage (RA-GZRS).
